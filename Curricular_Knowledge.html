Universal & Essential Supports for Access

MyEd dropdown labels for use in CBIEP Supports and Plans sections

AGENT INSTRUCTION: When populating the Supports and Plans section, select labels verbatim from this document. Universal Classroom Supports = available to any student. Essential Supports = identified through psycho-educational and/or medical testing and necessary for curriculum access.

How to use this reference

Universal Classroom Supports — select only Technology and Environmental items for CBIEP. Available to any student; no assessment required.
Essential Supports — identified through psycho-educational and/or medical testing. Necessary for the student to access the curriculum.
Bold labels in lists = exact MyEd dropdown text. Parenthetical text = examples/clarification only, not part of the label.
Universal first, Essential second. Supports are not people (EA support is not listed here).
Universal

Universal Classroom Supports for Access

Supports that could be made available to any student to foster greater inclusion in the classroom. For CBIEP, select Technology and Environmental items only.

Assessment / Response
  • Technology *must specify (Text-to-speech, Speech-to-text)
  • Alternatives to print (audiobooks, movies, videos, digital media etc.)
  • Oral instructions / reader
  • Use simplified language/directions
  • Sound field system in the classroom
  • Visual presentations of verbal material (word webs, visual organizers)
  • High-contrast materials with minimal visual clutter
  • Lesson outlines
  • Alternate ways of responding (oral, dictation, scribe, written, drawing, word processor, media etc.)
  • Separate settings
  • Additional time
  • Graphic organizers/Visual Supports
  • Use of a spelling dictionary, etc.
  • Use of a calculator, table of math facts, number line, manipulatives
  • Alternatives to assessments / essays (demonstrations, conferences, projects etc.)
Scheduling
  • Visual classroom schedule
  • Calendars with special events
  • Front-load schedule changes
  • Provide choice
  • Allow breaks
Content / Process
  • Reduce workload (fewer questions, shorter assignments etc.)
  • Reduce complexity of content (more concrete, simplified vocabulary etc.)
  • Increase complexity/abstractness of content
  • Allow self-selected content/opportunities to pursue individual interests
  • Flexible pacing
  • Alternatives to note-taking (scribe, audio recording, teacher notes provided, take a photo etc.)
Self-Management / Organization
  • Planner
  • Work organization system (colour coded files, binder etc.)
  • Classroom visual supports
Environmental
  • Preferential / flexible seating
  • Special lighting or acoustics
  • Sound field system
  • Good sight lines and placement and illumination to facilitate communication for oral and visual language
  • Sensory tools (fidget items, wiggle cushion, standing desks etc.)
  • Ability to move around indoor and outdoor spaces easily to access materials
  • Classroom zones/alternate work spaces
Social-Emotional
  • Calm space in the classroom
  • Check-ins
Instructional and Presentation
  • Text-to-speech and Speech-to-text
  • Alternatives to print (audiobooks, movies, videos, digital media etc.)
  • Oral instructions / reader
  • Use simplified language/directions
  • Sound field system in the classroom
  • Visual presentations of verbal material (word webs, visual organizers)
  • High-contrast materials with minimal visual clutter
  • Lesson outlines
  • Alternatives to note-taking (scribe, audio recording, teacher notes provided, take a photo etc.)
  • Provide captioning (open and closed captioning)
Timing
  • Additional time for tasks and assignments
  • Additional time to process oral information and directions
  • Use of a timer (visual, auditory) or countdown
Other
  • Free text field — specify as needed
Essential

Essential Supports for Access

Identified through psycho-educational and/or medical testing. Necessary for the student to access the curriculum.

Assessment / Response
  • Alternate formats (Braille, specialized assistive technology)
  • Alternative and Augmentative Communication Device (Talking Board, PECS, Hip Talk, Big Mac, Step-by-Step, PODDS, Touch Chat, Proloquo2go, Lamp Words for Life, Let me Talk, Eye Gaze etc.)
  • Signing
  • Assessment over several timed sessions
  • Hand held microphone for RMT (Remote Microphone Technology)
Scheduling
  • Personal visual schedule
  • Work/Break schedule
  • Scheduled sensory/movement breaks
  • Choice zone
Content / Process
  • Acceleration
  • Interactions with peers who have similar ability
Self-Management / Organization
  • Work organization system (basket system)
  • Personalized Visual or tactile supports
  • Learning Contract
  • Personal communication intent dictionary
Environmental
  • Orientation and Mobility Support (lift, walker, standing frame, cane, GPS etc.)
  • Specialized Seating (Source of sound to stronger ear, individual wedges etc.)
  • Alternative personal work space
  • Specialized equipment (Slant board, switch interface, Powerlink, audio hub etc.)
  • Positive Behaviour Support Plan (student safety plan)
  • Employee Risk Reduction Plan (employee safety plan)
Social-Emotional
  • Scheduled Check-ins
Instructional and Presentation
  • Designated reader or scribe
  • Personal hearing aid(s)
  • Personal FM/RMT (Remote Microphone Technology) system
  • ASL Interpreter
  • Alternative formats (Braille, large print, auditory, specialized assistive technology)
  • Low vision tools (monoculars and magnifiers)
Timing
  • Personal countdown script/visual timer
  • Personal visual timer
Other
  • Service dog

Agent notes — sourced from training document

  • Universal first, Essential second — only escalate to Essential when identified through assessment
  • Supports are not people — EA or staff support is not listed here and should not be entered as a support item
  • Choice – learning preferences – linked to supports (and to strategies)
  • Some categories are from CAST — inclusive practice based on needs, not domains
  • When writing goals and objectives, consider which supports may require explicit instruction
  • Strategies versus supports are distinct — do not conflate them in the CBIEP output
